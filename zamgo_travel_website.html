Quick View

Paseo del Mar in Zamboanga City is a popular waterfront promenade offering beautiful sunset views, a variety of food stalls, and a place for recreation and relaxation, near attractions like Fort Pilar and the tourism office.

Fort Pilar, a 17th-century Spanish military fortress in Zamboanga City, is now a major landmark and a regional museum of the National Museum of the Philippines, also serving as a Marian shrine dedicated to Our Lady of the Pillar.

Great Santa Cruz Island, located in Zamboanga City, Philippines, is famous for its unique pink sand beaches caused by pulverized red organ-pipe coral mixed with white sand, making it a popular tourist destination.

Merloquet Falls, located near Sibulao in Zamboanga, is a picturesque waterfall known for its stunning cascades and lush surroundings, offering a tranquil escape from the city. It's accessible by a scenic drive and a short, well-maintained trek, suitable for all fitness levels.

Pasonanca Natural Park, a 17,414-hectare protected area in the Zamboanga Peninsula, is a biodiversity hotspot and the 52nd ASEAN Heritage Park, known for its diverse flora and fauna, including rare species like the Mindanao Bleeding-Heart and the Philippine Eagle.

The Zamboanga City Hall, a historical landmark in the heart of Zamboanga City, serves as the seat of the local government and showcases Filipino colonial architecture.

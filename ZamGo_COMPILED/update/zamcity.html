Popular Delicacies

Curacha with Alavar Sauce

"Curacha" refers to the spanner crab, Ranina ranina, a red-colored crab found in tropical and subtropical waters, and is a regional specialty in some parts of the Philippines, particularly in the Zamboanga and Sulu regions.


Curacha is a popular crab species found in Zamboanga, best enjoyed with Alavar sauce, a rich, savory coconut-based sauce.

Satti

Satti is a popular dish of skewered, grilled meat and a sweet and spicy sauce that's a staple in Zamboanga City, Philippines. It's often eaten for breakfast, but can be enjoyed at any time of day.


Satti is a famous breakfast dish in Zamboanga, featuring skewered meat served with spicy, flavorful sauce and sticky rice.

Knickerbocker

Knickerbocker is an ice cream sundae dessert from Zamboanga City, Philippines made up of various fresh fruit chunks, flavored gulaman (agar) cubes, and nata de coco in condensed milk topped with strawberry ice cream.


Knickerbocker is Zamboanga’s refreshing dessert made of fruits, jelly, and sweetened milk, similar to halo-halo but creamier.

Crafts and Textiles

Yakan Weaving Village

The Yakan weaving tradition, known for its vibrant geometric patterns, originated among the Yakan people, an indigenous Muslim tribe from the island of Basilan in the Sulu Archipelago, southern Philippines.

Tausug Pis Syabit

The Tausug pis syabit is a vibrant, square, handwoven cloth tapestry, traditionally worn by Tausug men as a head covering or accessory, symbolizing status and cultural identity, and featuring intricate geometric patterns.
